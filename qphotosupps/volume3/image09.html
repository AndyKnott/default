Surrounded by admiring folk fans, a young Bob Dylan makes an unexpected appearance at the Singers' Club Christmas party in London, 1962, on his first visit to Britain. Declining to hang around and sink a pint or three of real ale, he performed a couple of numbers, to an audience including Ewan MacColl and Peggy Seeger, and buggered off.
